What is DevOps?

DevOps is a set of practices that combine software development (Dev) and IT operations (Ops) to shorten the development lifecycle and deliver high-quality software continuously. It aims to improve collaboration and productivity by automating infrastructure, workflows, and continuously measuring application performance. Key aspects of DevOps include continuous integration, continuous delivery, automated testing, and monitoring. By fostering a culture of collaboration between development and operations teams, DevOps helps organisations accelerate delivery, improve reliability, and better respond to customer needs.

Benefits of DevOps

Adopting DevOps practices brings numerous advantages to businesses across various sectors. Some benefits include:

  • Faster Delivery of Features
  • Improved Collaboration and Communication
  • Enhanced Efficiency through Automation
  • Greater Reliability and Stability
  • Continuous Feedback and Improvement
  • Reduced Costs and Increased ROI
